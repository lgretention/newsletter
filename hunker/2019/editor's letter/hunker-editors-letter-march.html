A Letter From Eve

Dear You Guys: I’m writing this, the first of our new monthly editor’s letters, in my favorite chair at Hunker House, our new IRL outpost in Venice Beach, California.

We created the space to answer a question that’s been on our minds: If Hunker were a house, what kind of house would it be? And, well, we couldn’t be happier with it. Hunker House not only embodies our love of beautiful, functional design, it’s also a place for storytelling, hosting, and collaborating with people and brands we love.

And we gave the space a good workout in February. We partnered with amazing folks like Blu Dot, Floyd, Hem, Armadillo, Ettitude, Year and Day, Poketo, and others. We celebrated Black History Month with a visit from Roe and E of @brownkids and an amazing panel featuring superstars Justina Blakeney, Cococozy, and Maurice Harris, moderated by Elvis Mitchell. We unboxed stuff.
"Hunker House not only embodies our love of beautiful, functional design, it’s also a place for storytelling, hosting, and collaborating with people and brands we love."

For more, check out the Hunker House hub for all the latest happenings, products, and guest stays.
In Other News…
  • If you haven’t watched Russian Doll, please do that right away. Then read Leo’s super-smart Q&A with the show’s production designer, Michael Bricker, to learn more about the amazing interiors.
  • Landscape architect Mia Lehrer is a one-of-a-kind lady whose vision is evident throughout the Los Angeles area, from stadiums to parks to the L.A. River. Deanna’s interview with her is charming, whip-smart, and inspiring.
  • Any fan of design felt the loss of Karl Lagerfeld last month. Did you know he’d also recently branched out into home design with a limited-edition collection of neoclassical/postmodern/very heavy-looking pieces crafted from rare marble? Architectural Digest first told us of it in October.
  • Apropos of nothing: After re-watching Crazy Rich Asians for the 5th time, I re-read China Rich Girlfriend, because preparedness. And for those of you who, like me, wonder if the Baos’ drive-in car elevator is a thing in real life, It totally is.
  • Finally, for all you Scandi-heads (read: EVERYONE), we’ve launched Scandi-land, a hub dedicated to brands, homes, topics, and news about Scandinavian design. Have at it, and check back for updates. You’re welcome.

That’s all for now. Happy March!

Xx Eve
