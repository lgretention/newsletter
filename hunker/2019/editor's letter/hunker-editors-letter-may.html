Editor's Note

EDITION NO. 3

Friends:

Around here, it’s pretty much our job to celebrate creative people and stuff they make. But there’s something about springtime that cranks up my appreciation of these folks — people who commit their hearts, hands, voices and minds to provoking thought and inspiration for the rest of us.

Here are just a few of the recent visits we’ve had with some of my favorite creative souls...

Frances Anderton, host of KCRW’s popular DnA - Design and Architecture show and podcast, just did a fascinating segment on the restoration of Notre Dame that explores the oft-thorny cultural, social, religious, and architectural forces that converge on and around an iconic building. Frances’s infectious curiosity, expansive knowledge, and super-classy accent have the power to spark a passion for design in just about anyone. I was so excited when she welcomed writer Teena Apeles and photographer Stephen Paul to the eclectic, music-centric apartment she shares with her husband, Bennett Stein. It’s in a Frank Gehry-designed building — naturally — and it’s truly one of a kind.

Mike Perry, a visual artist whose work you’ll recognize from the Broad City credits, gave us a tour of his Crown Heights studio, where he creates his joyfully psychedelic work. "Nothing's better than buying a bunch of fluorescent inks,” he shares with writer Laura Lambert, and we couldn’t agree more. (I’m seriously eyeing his “Forward Looking” limited-edition print.)

Ceramicist Helen Levi gave Elizabeth Stamp and photographer Chloe Berk a peek inside her studio as well, where she creates one-of-a-kind pieces featuring abstract elements such as swirls and paint-spatters. The light-filled Brooklyn space, which Levi’s architect sister helped to set up, is filled with her creations at various stages of completion and inspires awe at the mixture of scale and artistry she achieves there. Oh, and just FYI, I’m pretty obsessed with Levi’s meadow planters (and my birthday is in just a few days...anyone...?)

That’s all for me. Have a great month, everyone!

Eve
